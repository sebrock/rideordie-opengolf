# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Ride or Die Scorecard to be a serious, useful, and welcoming open-source project. Contributors, maintainers, users, sponsors, and community members should be able to participate without harassment, intimidation, or bad-faith behavior.

We pledge to make participation in this project respectful and productive for everyone, regardless of experience level, age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity or orientation, or technical background.

## Our standards

Behavior that helps the project:

- Giving clear, constructive feedback
- Assuming good intent while still being precise
- Asking questions before making accusations
- Respecting different levels of experience
- Keeping criticism focused on the work, not the person
- Helping newcomers find suitable first issues
- Documenting decisions so others can follow
- Respecting accessibility needs
- Being transparent about conflicts of interest

Behavior that is not acceptable:

- Harassment, threats, or intimidation
- Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces
- Personal attacks or insults
- Trolling or deliberate disruption
- Publishing private information without consent
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions
- Spamming, scams, or deceptive promotion
- Abuse of contributor, maintainer, or sponsor status
- Encouraging illegal activity

## Bitcoin, Nostr, and sponsorship discussions

This project sits inside the Bitcoin and Nostr ecosystem, where opinions can be strong. Strong technical opinions are fine. Gatekeeping, purity tests, personal attacks, and harassment are not.

Sponsors and ecosystem projects may be discussed as potential collaborators, but contribution decisions should remain transparent and aligned with the project’s open-source-first principles.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Discussions
- Chat channels
- Project calls
- Event spaces connected to the project
- Social media interactions when representing the project

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action when behavior harms the project or its participants. Possible actions include:

1. Private clarification or warning
2. Public moderation note
3. Temporary restriction from project spaces
4. Removal from contributor roles
5. Ban from project spaces

Serious threats, harassment, or illegal behavior may be escalated immediately.

## Reporting

To report a conduct issue, contact the maintainers through the support or maintainer contact channel listed in the repository.

Include:

- What happened
- Where it happened
- Who was involved
- Links, screenshots, or timestamps if available
- Whether immediate action is needed

Reports will be handled with discretion. Retaliation against someone who makes a good-faith report is not allowed.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers are expected to:

- Apply this Code of Conduct consistently
- Avoid conflicts of interest when moderating
- Protect reporters from retaliation
- Explain moderation decisions where appropriate
- Keep the project focused and safe for productive work

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant and adjusted for this project’s open-source, Bitcoin, Nostr, and event-platform context.
